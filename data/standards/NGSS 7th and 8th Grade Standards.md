## NGSS 7th Grade Science Standards

**Earth & Space Science (ESS):**

- MS‑ESS1‑4: Construct a scientific explanation based on evidence from rock strata for using the geologic time scale to organize Earth's 4.6‑billion‑year history.
- MS‑ESS2‑2: Construct an explanation with evidence for how geoscience processes have changed Earth's surface across time and spatial scales.
- MS‑ESS3‑3: Apply scientific principles to design a method for monitoring and minimizing human environmental impact.
- MS‑ESS3‑4: Construct an evidence‑based argument on how rising human population and consumption affect Earth's systems.
- MS‑ESS3‑5: Ask questions to clarify evidence of factors causing the rise in global temperatures over the past century.

**Life Science (LS):**

- MS‑LS1‑2: Develop and use a model to describe cell function and how parts contribute to it.
- MS‑LS1‑3: Use evidence‑based arguments to explain the body as interacting systems of cells.
- MS‑LS1‑5: Construct scientific explanations for how environmental and genetic factors influence organism growth.
- MS‑LS1‑7: Model how food is rearranged via chemical reactions to support growth or release energy in organisms.
- MS‑LS3‑1: Model how gene mutations affect proteins and potentially organism structure and function.
- MS‑LS3‑2: Model why asexual reproduction produces identical offspring, while sexual reproduction leads to genetic variation. 

**Physical Science (PS):**

- MS‑PS1‑1: Develop models to describe atomic composition of simple molecules and extended structures.
- MS‑PS1‑2: Analyze data to determine if a chemical reaction occurred based on properties before and after interactions.

### NGSS 8th Grade Science Standards

**Earth & Space Science (ESS):**

- MS‑ESS1‑1: Model the Earth–Sun–Moon system to explain lunar phases, eclipses, and seasons.    
- MS‑ESS1‑2: Model the role of gravity in motions within galaxies and the solar system.
- MS‑ESS1‑3: Analyze and interpret data to determine scale properties of solar system objects. 

**Life Science (LS):**

- MS‑LS1‑4: Use empirical evidence and reasoning to explain how animal behaviors and plant structures affect reproductive success.
- MS‑LS4‑1: Analyze patterns in the fossil record to document life's history, diversity, extinction, and change.
- MS‑LS4‑2: Apply scientific reasoning to explain anatomical differences and similarities among modern and fossil organisms.
- MS‑LS4‑3: Compare embryological development across species to infer relationships.
- MS‑LS4‑4: Explain how genetic variations increase or decrease survival probabilities in specific environments.
- MS‑LS4‑5: Gather and synthesize info about technologies influencing inheritance of desired traits in organisms.
- MS‑LS4‑6: Use mathematical representations to explain how natural selection affects trait prevalence over time.

**Physical Science (PS):**

- MS‑PS2‑1: Apply Newton’s Third Law to design a solution involving collision motion.
- MS‑PS2‑2: Plan an investigation showing how sum of forces and mass affect motion.
- MS‑PS2‑4: Argue, using evidence, that gravitational interactions are attractive and depend on mass.
- MS‑PS3‑1: Create and interpret graphs showing relationships between kinetic energy, mass, and speed.
- MS‑PS4‑1: Use math to model how wave amplitude relates to energy.
